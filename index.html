Human-Centered AI Support for Immersive Data Sensemaking

Bringing together HCI, AI, visualization & cognitive science researchers

@ NordiCHI 2026 | October 3-4, 2026

Overview

Immersive analytics and extended reality environments are creating new ways for people to explore complex data, reason spatially, and collaborate around emerging patterns. At the same time, rapid progress in generative AI, adaptive interfaces, and multimodal sensing is changing what these environments can do: AI systems can summarize, recommend, explain, annotate, simulate, and even take initiative during sensemaking. The opportunity is substantial, but so is the risk of building systems that are technically impressive yet poorly aligned with human goals, judgment, agency, and trust.

Motivation

Human-centered AI support for immersive data sensemaking (HCAI4IDS) asks how AI can become a useful collaborator in immersive data work without obscuring uncertainty, narrowing interpretation, or displacing human expertise. Researchers and practitioners need shared language for designing these systems, evaluating them, and understanding when AI should guide, explain, adapt, or step back.

Workshop Focus

The workshop will explore design perspectives, theoretical guidelines, multimodal human-AI understanding, adaptive AI collaborators, initiative and agency, trust calibration, novel application areas, and evaluation approaches for HCAI-supported immersive systems. We are especially interested in work that connects technical AI capabilities with the lived experience of users making sense of complex data.

Guiding Questions

  • How should immersive systems support joint sensemaking between individuals, groups, and AI agents?
  • What forms of explanation, recommendation, and adaptation help users reason with AI output rather than simply accept it?
  • How can speech, gesture, gaze, affective signals, and interaction traces help AI systems understand user intent while respecting privacy and autonomy?
  • When should an AI collaborator lead, follow, challenge, or remain in the background during analysis?
  • How should we evaluate trust, agency, insight, collaboration quality, and cognitive load in immersive human-AI workflows?

Format and Goals

The half-day workshop is designed as a working forum rather than a sequence of isolated presentations. Participants will share short extended abstract presentations, take part in theme-based group work, and contribute to a synthesis discussion that identifies open challenges and promising research directions.

  • Map the field by clarifying key concepts, design tensions, and methodological gaps in HCAI-supported immersive data sensemaking.
  • Connect communities across HCI, AI, visualization, immersive analytics, cognitive science, and applied domains where complex data sensemaking matters.
  • Develop research directions for human-centered AI collaborators that remain interpretable, accountable, adaptive, and respectful of user agency.
  • Build lasting collaboration through a shared research agenda and a planned synthesis article after the workshop.

Workshop Outcomes

Publications

Extended abstracts, presentation slides, and photographs will be made available on the workshop website. A synthesized extended article focusing on workshop outcomes will be published in a venue to be determined after the workshop.

Research Agenda

Theme-based group work will be synthesized into a comprehensive research agenda for HCAI4IDS, identifying key challenges and opportunities for future research.

Networking

A key objective is to foster new Nordic and international networks and opportunities for future collaborations, including new research project initiatives and joint publications.
